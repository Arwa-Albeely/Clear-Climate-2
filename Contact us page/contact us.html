Frequently Asked Questions

Climate change is a significant long-term adjustment in the usual atmospheric condition. The atmospheric conditions like temperature and precipitation become extreme, leading to an unusually dry or wet atmosphere.

Global warming is the increase in the emission of greenhouse gases which causes a rise in the global average atmospheric conditions.

What Is The Difference Between Climate And Weather? Climate involves the weather condition of a wide area over a given length of time whereas weather is the atmospheric condition observed within a short period in a small location.

Climate change is a threat to human health and overall well-being. Here are some of the health effects of climate change. ● An increase in temperature as a result of climate change causes heat-related diseases which can lead to death. Pregnant women, children and the elderly are predisposed to this risk. ● The rise in temperature and terrible weather conditions can adversely affect the environmental conditions which lead to drought, flood and wide fires. It further affects the availability of food.
